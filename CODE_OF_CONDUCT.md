# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We are committed to making participation in this project a respectful, welcoming, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, constructive, and patient.
- Focus criticism on ideas, code, data, methods, and documentation rather than people.
- Give and receive feedback in good faith.
- Be mindful that wearable-sensor, biomechanics, health, and clinical interpretation topics can affect real people and communities.
- Credit sources, datasets, papers, and prior implementations when you use them.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Personal attacks, trolling, or sustained disruption of project spaces.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Submitting credentials, private participant data, confidential records, or unlawfully obtained information.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make someone feel unsafe or unwelcome.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying expectations and may remove comments, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct. Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation for repeated or severe violations.

To report a concern, contact the project maintainers through a private channel when available. If a concern involves a security vulnerability, leaked credential, or sensitive data, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead of opening a public issue.

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the Contributor Covenant, version 2.1.
